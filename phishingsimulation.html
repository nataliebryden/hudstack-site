Test. Measure. Improve.

Phishing Simulations

Test your team's awareness with realistic, controlled phishing simulations. Measure results. Track improvement.

Get Started
What it is

Real simulations. Real insights. No real harm.

We send realistic but harmless simulated phishing emails to your team and measure how they respond.

Who opens them? Who clicks? Who reports them correctly? You get a detailed report showing exactly where your team stands, and where they need targeted training to improve.

The process

How It Works

01

We set up your campaign

We work with you to choose the right phishing templates for your industry. Fake invoices, password resets, CEO emails, delivery notifications. All customised to look realistic.

02

We send the simulations

Your team receives simulated phishing emails over a set period. Everything is tracked securely. Your team doesn't know it's a test until after the results are in.

03

We measure the results

Our platform tracks opens, clicks, and reports. You get a branded dashboard showing your team's performance, broken down by department or group.

04

We help you improve

Based on the results, we recommend targeted training to close the gaps. Run another simulation later to measure improvement over time.

Deliverables

What You Get

  • Branded dashboard with real-time results
  • Team performance breakdown by department or group
  • Individual awareness scoring (kept confidential)
  • Detailed PDF report for leadership
  • Recommendations for targeted training
  • Before-and-after comparison when you re-test
The case for simulation

Why It Matters

Over 90% of cyberattacks begin with a phishing email.

Regular simulations are the only way to measure whether your team can spot them, and the only way to prove improvement over time. Without testing, you're relying on hope. With simulations, you have data.

Our simulations give leadership the visibility they need to make informed decisions about training investments, and give employees a safe environment to learn from real mistakes before a real attack occurs.
